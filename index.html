FAQ

Basic Creating&Designing Scanning&Printing
What Kind of Information Can Be Stored in a QR Code?

QR codes are incredibly versatile and can store a wide range of information. This includes URLs to open websites instantly, contact details like name, phone number, and email, as well as plain text messages. They can even store Wi-Fi credentials, making it easy for customers to connect to your network with a simple scan. The possibilities are endless for what can be embedded in a QR code.

Can QR Code Scans Be Tracked?

Yes, it is possible to track QR code scans, including where, when, and how often they are scanned. Additionally, you can see which devices and operating systems were used for the scan. This data is valuable for measuring the success of marketing campaigns and understanding user behavior.

Do QR Codes Expire?

Static QR codes do not expire. They will remain functional indefinitely, as long as the information they contain (e.g., a URL) remains unchanged. If you want more flexibility, such as the ability to update content or track scans, you can opt for dynamic QR codes. These codes are also permanent but allow content to be modified after generation.

What is the Difference Between Static and Dynamic QR Codes?

Static QR codes are fixed once created and cannot be edited or tracked. They are best for simple applications where the content doesnâ€™t need to change. Dynamic QR codes, on the other hand, are more flexible and offer the ability to update the embedded content (such as links or text) after printing. Dynamic codes also allow for tracking and analytics, making them ideal for marketing campaigns.

How Do I Manage a QR Code with a QR Code Generator?

After signing up for a QR code generator, you can use it to create and manage both static and dynamic QR codes. You can access features such as adding logos or branding to your codes, tracking scan statistics, and even setting up custom short URLs. The platform might offer a trial period to explore these features before committing to a paid version

Is it Possible to Create Different QR Codes for the Same Content?

Yes, QR codes are highly customizable, and there are numerous versions available for any given content. With 40 different QR code versions, four error correction levels, and eight possible mask designs, there are up to 1280 possible combinations for a single input. However, for marketing purposes, only versions 1-7 are commonly used.

Can I Customize a QR Code with My Company Logo or Icon?

Yes, it is possible to incorporate your companyâ€™s logo or icon into the center of the QR code to make it more branded and visually appealing. This customization is often done for marketing purposes to make the QR code stand out.

Do QR Codes Have to Be Black and White?

No, QR codes do not need to be black and white. You can customize the colors to match your brand's colors or choose any color scheme you prefer. There are over 16 million possible color combinations using RGB notation, allowing for vibrant and personalized designs.

Can a QR Code Be Edited or Changed?

Yes, you can change the content of a dynamic QR code after it has been printed or distributed. For example, if you need to correct a typo or change the destination link, you can do so without reprinting the QR code. This flexibility is available with a pro version of a QR code generator.

How Do I Generate a Large Batch of QR Codes?

Some QR code generators allow you to create large batches of codes in one go. This is useful if you need to generate a series of QR codes for a specific campaign or event.

What Graphic Formats Do the Generated QR Codes Support?

QR codes can be downloaded in high-resolution JPG format. If you need other formats like PNG, SVG, or EPS, you will need to sign up for an account. Note that EPS formats are typically available in black and white only.

What Should I Know Before Printing My QR Code?

Before printing your QR code, it is important to consider factors such as the appropriate size, contrasting colors, and overall clarity. A blurry or poorly contrasted code might be unreadable. Always test your QR code thoroughly to ensure it works before printing it for distribution.

How Do I Read or Scan a QR Code?

On most smartphones, simply open the camera app and point it at the QR code. The phone will automatically recognize the code and show a notification. If the scan doesnâ€™t trigger, check the settings to ensure QR code scanning is enabled. Alternatively, you can install a third-party QR code reader app from your app store.

Can QR Codes Be Scanned Without an App?

Yes, most iOS and Android smartphones now have QR code scanning functionality built directly into the camera app. You don't need to download a separate app to scan QR codes unless your phone doesn't have this feature.

Is There a Scan Limit for QR Codes?

No, there is no scan limit for QR codes. They can be scanned an unlimited number of times, making them perfect for widespread use in marketing and customer engagement.
